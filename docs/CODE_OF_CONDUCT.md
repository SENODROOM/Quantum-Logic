# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

**Be Respectful and Inclusive**
- Treat all individuals with respect and dignity
- Welcome newcomers and help them learn our community norms
- Value different perspectives and experiences
- Use inclusive language and avoid assumptions about others

**Be Collaborative**
- Work together to solve problems and build great software
- Listen carefully to different viewpoints and consider alternative approaches
- Share knowledge and help others learn
- Give and receive constructive feedback gracefully

**Be Professional**
- Maintain a professional and courteous demeanor in all interactions
- Focus on what is best for the community and the project
- Respect privacy and confidentiality of sensitive information
- Follow project guidelines and processes

**Be Considerate**
- Think about how your words and actions will affect others
- Be patient with those who may have different levels of experience
- Consider the impact of your contributions on the broader community
- Take responsibility for your mistakes and learn from them

### Prohibited Behavior

**Harassment**
- Unwelcome comments regarding a person's appearance, body, or lifestyle
- Deliberate misgendering or use of dead names
- Sexual images or content in public spaces
- Inappropriate physical contact or unwelcome sexual attention
- Stalking or following someone without their consent

**Discrimination**
- Discriminatory jokes, language, or imagery
- Dismissive attitudes toward concerns about discrimination
- Exclusion from community activities based on identity
- Creating barriers to participation for certain groups

**Disruptive Behavior**
- Personal attacks, insults, or demeaning comments
- Trolling or intentionally provoking negative reactions
- Publishing private information without permission
- Spamming or flooding communication channels

**Unacceptable Conduct**
- Threats of violence or incitement of harm
- Harassment in any form (online or offline)
- Sharing explicit content without consent
- Vandalism or deliberate destruction of community resources
- Any illegal activities or violations of intellectual property rights

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all community spaces, including but not limited to:

- **Online Platforms**: GitHub repositories, Discord servers, Slack channels, forums
- **Events**: Conferences, meetups, workshops, hackathons
- **Communications**: Emails, direct messages, video calls, chat messages
- **Contributions**: Code reviews, pull requests, issue discussions, documentation
- **Social Media**: Interactions related to the project on any platform

This applies to both project maintainers and community members, regardless of their role or level of contribution.

## Enforcement

### Reporting Guidelines

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to the project maintainers. We take all reports seriously and will respond appropriately.

**How to Report:**
1. **Email**: conduct@quantumlogics.io
2. **GitHub**: Send a private message to any project maintainer
3. **Discord**: Message any moderator or admin privately
4. **In-Person Events**: Speak with event organizers or staff

**What to Include in Your Report:**
- Your contact information (optional, but helpful for follow-up)
- Description of the incident
- Date, time, and location of the incident
- Names of individuals involved (if known)
- Any witnesses to the incident
- Screenshots or other evidence (if available)
- Any other relevant information

### Investigation Process

1. **Initial Response**: We will acknowledge receipt of your report within 24 hours
2. **Investigation**: We will investigate the report thoroughly and confidentially
3. **Action**: We will take appropriate action based on the findings
4. **Follow-up**: We will inform you of the outcome (within privacy constraints)

### Consequences

Unacceptable behavior from any community member, including sponsors and those with decision-making authority, will not be tolerated. Anyone asked to stop unacceptable behavior is expected to comply immediately.

**Possible Consequences:**
- **Warning**: Private or public warning about the behavior
- **Temporary Suspension**: Temporary removal from community spaces
- **Permanent Ban**: Permanent removal from all community spaces
- **Legal Action**: Involvement of law enforcement for illegal activities
- **Public Statement**: Public acknowledgment of the incident and resolution

The severity of the consequence will depend on the nature and impact of the behavior, previous warnings, and other relevant factors.

## Community Guidelines

### Communication Guidelines

**Written Communication**
- Use clear, concise language
- Avoid sarcasm and humor that could be misunderstood
- Use appropriate formatting (code blocks, lists) for readability
- Consider cultural differences in communication styles

**Code Reviews**
- Focus on the code, not the person
- Provide specific, actionable feedback
- Acknowledge good work and improvements
- Be patient with contributors who are learning

**Issue Discussions**
- Stay on topic and relevant to the issue
- Provide context and examples when reporting problems
- Search existing issues before creating new ones
- Use appropriate labels and templates

### Contribution Guidelines

**Code Contributions**
- Follow the project's coding standards and conventions
- Write clear, descriptive commit messages
- Include tests for new functionality
- Update documentation as needed

**Documentation**
- Write clear, comprehensive documentation
- Use inclusive language and examples
- Consider different skill levels when writing tutorials
- Keep documentation up to date with code changes

**Community Support**
- Help answer questions from newcomers
- Share knowledge and resources
- Be patient with repetitive questions
- Direct people to appropriate resources when needed

## Diversity and Inclusion

### Our Commitment

We are committed to creating a diverse and inclusive community where everyone feels welcome and valued. We believe that diverse perspectives lead to better outcomes and a stronger community.

### Initiatives

**Outreach Programs**
- Mentorship programs for underrepresented groups
- Scholarships for conference attendance
- Community partnerships with diversity-focused organizations
- Outreach to educational institutions in diverse communities

**Accessibility**
- Ensure our tools and platforms are accessible to people with disabilities
- Provide alternative formats for content when needed
- Consider accessibility in all design decisions
- Regular accessibility audits and improvements

**Inclusive Language**
- Use gender-neutral language in documentation and communications
- Avoid idioms and cultural references that may not be universally understood
- Provide translations when resources allow
- Be mindful of time zones and cultural differences

## Conflict Resolution

### Dispute Resolution Process

1. **Direct Communication**: Try to resolve conflicts directly with the other party first
2. **Mediation**: If direct communication fails, involve a neutral third party
3. **Formal Process**: For serious conflicts, follow the formal reporting process
4. **Escalation**: If necessary, involve senior maintainers or external mediators

### Guidelines for Healthy Disagreement

- **Assume Good Intent**: Start from the assumption that others have good intentions
- **Focus on Issues**: Address the problem, not the person
- **Listen Actively**: Make an effort to understand others' perspectives
- **Find Common Ground**: Look for areas of agreement and build from there
- **Know When to Disengage**: Take breaks when discussions become heated

## Maintainer Responsibilities

### Leadership Commitment

Project maintainers are expected to:

- **Lead by Example**: Demonstrate the behavior expected from community members
- **Enforce Standards**: Apply this Code of Conduct fairly and consistently
- **Respond Promptly**: Address reports and concerns in a timely manner
- **Provide Support**: Help community members navigate conflicts and issues
- **Continuous Learning**: Stay educated about diversity, equity, and inclusion

### Decision Making

Maintainers will:

- **Make Transparent Decisions**: Explain the reasoning behind important decisions
- **Seek Community Input**: Involve the community in significant changes
- **Document Processes**: Maintain clear guidelines and procedures
- **Review Regularly**: Periodically review and update this Code of Conduct
